How to use this crypto margin calculator

This free crypto margin calculator is built specifically for Hyperliquid perpetual contracts. Enter your trade parameters and get instant results — no sign-up required.

  1. Select your asset (BTC, ETH, SOL, ARB, or other)
  2. Choose your direction — long or short

  4. Input your wallet balance (used for cross margin liquidation calculation)
  5. Set your leverage with the slider or quick preset buttons
  6. Optionally enter a target and stop-loss price to see estimated PnL and risk/reward
Cross margin

Cross margin mode

Your entire wallet balance acts as collateral across all positions. Losses from one position can be covered by gains in another, but a large losing position can drain your whole balance.

Isolated margin

Isolated margin mode

Only the margin you assign to a position is at risk. If liquidated, the rest of your wallet is safe. Ideal for higher-risk trades where you want to limit downside exposure.

Margin and liquidation price formulas

Understanding how your crypto margin calculator works helps you manage risk more effectively. Here are the core formulas used for Hyperliquid perps:

Required margin

Required Margin = Notional Value ÷ Leverage
Where Notional Value = Entry Price × Position Size

Liquidation price — isolated long

Liquidation Price = Entry Price − (Allocated Margin − Maintenance Margin − Fee) ÷ Position Size

Liquidation price — isolated short

Liquidation Price = Entry Price + (Allocated Margin − Maintenance Margin − Fee) ÷ Position Size

ROE (Return on Equity)

ROE % = PnL ÷ Required Margin × 100
This is why a 10x leveraged trade moving 5% in your favor gives a 50% ROE on margin used.

Frequently asked questions

A crypto margin calculator helps traders determine how much collateral (margin) is required to open a leveraged position, at what price their position will be liquidated, how large a position they can open with their available balance, and the estimated profit or loss at a given target or stop-loss price.
In cross margin mode, your entire wallet balance acts as collateral for all open positions. This reduces liquidation risk for any single position but means a losing trade can drain your entire balance. In isolated margin mode, only the margin you allocate to a specific position is at risk — if liquidated, your other funds remain safe.
For isolated margin long positions: Liquidation Price = Entry Price − (Allocated Margin − Maintenance Margin − Trading Fee) ÷ Position Size. For short positions, the formula is reversed. Hyperliquid uses a maintenance margin rate of approximately 0.5% and a taker fee of 0.035%.
Hyperliquid supports up to 50x leverage on major perpetual contracts like BTC and ETH, and varying maximum leverage on other assets. Higher leverage dramatically increases both potential profit and liquidation risk — always use a crypto margin calculator to know your exact liquidation level before entering a trade.
ROE stands for Return on Equity. In leveraged crypto trading, ROE measures your profit or loss as a percentage of the margin you put in — not the total notional value. A 10x leveraged position that moves 5% in your favor gives a 50% ROE, even though the asset only moved 5%.
This crypto margin calculator uses Hyperliquid's standard maintenance margin rate (~0.5%) and taker fee (~0.035%) for its formulas. Results are close approximations for educational use. Always verify exact margin requirements on the Hyperliquid platform before placing real trades, as rates may vary by asset or be updated by the exchange.
